Simple Ascii Reference

ASCII is a binary-encoding standard for mapping characters to binary code, through it's 7 Bit format it can display up to 128 unique characters and rappresents the de-facto standard for simple english characters in computers.

Nowadays the most popular UTF-8 encoding standardizes more characters using up to 4 bytes(32 bits), but it preserves the 128 ascii characters in the first byte.

The Ascii chart-code is formed by 32 control characters and 96 print related characters; since this standard is an evolution from telegraph and morse code most of the initial control characters, which are related to specific telex functionalities, are not used in computers.

Nevertheless it is the format that was choosen for the development of the first computers (in the US around 1960), and that subsequently tookover the worldwide infotech industry.
